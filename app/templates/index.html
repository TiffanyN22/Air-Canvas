About

A timeline of our project.

  • Week One

    _____

    Our team started by learning about machine learning, neural networks, and the applications of the technology. Soon after, we began brainstorming project ideas. After some careful debate and consideration, we settled on Air Canvas, a webcam-based art application. Air Canvas began as a convolutional neural network trained on internet stock images. However, we promptly realized that this approach was flawed. There was one simple error: the internet data didn't match our webcam data. We realized that our webcam-based service required webcam-based data, so we began building a new dataset. Entering the weekend, each team member was assigned the task of collecting and annotating 130 webcam images. Air Canvas truly is a product of hard work.

  • Week Two

    _____

    Now that we have the webcam images labeled in our dataset, we used Roboflow's data augmentation feature to get more data, avoid overfitting, and account for different webcam environments. For example, we messed with the brightness and saturation to account for different lighting conditions, added noise to defend against adversarial attacks, flipped the images to have more of a mix between left and right hands, and more.

    Once we finished finalizing our dataset, we learned about Convolutional Neural Network and YOLO's algorithm before training our model with yolov5. Afterwards, we used weights and biases to evaluate our model's accuracy, precision, and recall.

    Next up, we started working on the canvas and webcam, which involved using cv2 to draw circles with different sizes and colors at a given coordinate, passing our webcam frames into our trained model to detect the various hand signals along with their coordinates and classes, drawing a bounding box around the gestures with cv2, and writing code to adjust our brush based on the detected hand signal.

    Finally, we got started with the frontend of our website, which includes picking a template and editing the information presented.

  • Week Three

    ____

    During Week Three, our team put our heads down and got to work on the website design aspect of the project. We learned HTML, Flask, CSS, and other tools to accomplish this. Through much trial and error, we finally finished our website before the end of the week. We even had time to spare!

  • Finished Product

    _____

    By the end of all 3 weeks, we were able to successfully train a model to recognize the 5 gestures and change the brush or add color to the drawing accordingly. Furthermore, we wrote code to capture and process video from the user's webcam, created a canvas that displayed both the webcam and drawing, and coded in an option for the user to upload their own images for the background of the canvas. For the gestures, we also added bounding boxes for when the model recognizes the user's hand gestures, displayed the brush size when the user puts a thumbs up or thumbs down, and changed the brush color when the user has a fist along the left side on the canvas.

Object Detection

Air Canvas relies on computer vision. Our team used object detection technology to recognize the user’s input gesture. For example, our Convolutional Neural Networks (CNNs) are able to distinguish between a thumbs-up and a thumbs-down. There’s no human being making these decisions– it’s all done by computers!

YOLOv5

YOLOv5 provided much-needed speed and precision for our model. As our product is webcam based, we needed a CNN that was capable of keeping up with fast movements. YOLOv5’s “you only look once” technology allows our model to scan each frame a single time, instead of hundreds of times.

Dataset

Our dataset contains an 80%/20% split between training/testing images. Each image was collected and labeled manually by our team.

Roboflow

We used Roboflow to scrape data off the internet. In the end, we ended up replacing that data with our own webcam footage we collected manually. Adding bounding boxes with RoboFlow was the finishing touch, and our data was ready to be trained.

Webcam Integration

Air Canvas is a webcam-based project, meaning that users will need to connect cameras to use it. We primarily used Flask to get this technology to connect. It was by far the most difficult part of our project, and we learned a ton from completing it.
